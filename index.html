Your Role

Front Desk is the location. Client Services Coordinator is the responsibility.

You'll spend a lot of your time at the front counter, but this role is much more than answering phones and admin. You are the central point of coordination between members, families, instructors and leadership. Your job is to create a professional, welcoming experience that keeps members coming back - and frees the instructors up to teach and grow Kimekai.

Where this role goes This is a foundation role with a real career path:
Client Services Coordinator → Operations Coordinator → Dojo Operations Manager.
Build the habits in this handbook well and you grow with the dojo as it grows.

How this fits in. This is the practical, day-to-day "how we do it here" guide. It sits alongside three strategic documents from our client-services team - the 90-Day Onboarding Plan, the "What Good Looks Like" Playbook, and the Weekly CSC Dashboard. This handbook covers the operations; those cover the standard, the roadmap and the scorecard.

Welcoming a New Trial / Walk-In

First impressions matter - this is where the member journey starts.

  1. Get them to scan the QR code - the waiver comes through to our email automatically.
  2. Ask them to take off shoes and socks and wait for class.
  3. Introduce them to the instructors if available.
  4. Show them they bow at the mat before stepping on.

Signing Up a New Customer (Spark)

  1. Go to their Spark profile (Adrian sets up your login first).
  2. Click their email, then click Email templates.
  3. Choose their program - Thunderbolts / Young Samurai / Cadets / Adults.
  4. Select Promo or No Promo depending on what they're doing.
The Promo - $19.95 $19.95 = 2 weeks unlimited training + a belt + a Gi (uniform).
On the promo → pick the program's Promo template. Not on it → pick No Promo.
